 Winged Kuriboh  is  Jaden Yuki 's main  Spirit Partner . In the anime, he begins following Jaden after the latter received the " Winged Kuriboh " card from  Yugi Muto . In the manga, the card was instead given to Jaden by  Koyo Hibiki .

 Biography 
 Ancient Egypt 
       White Winged Kuriboh.     
 In the  manga , the priest before  Karim  judged  Tragoedia  using the  Millennium Scales . Winged Kuriboh, known as the White Feathered Spirit, was weighed against the  Feather of Ma'at . The White Feathered Spirit represented the sin in Tragoedia's heart, while the feather represented righteousness, truth and order. Tragoedia's sins greatly outweighed righteousness, truth and order, causing the White Feathered Spirit's end of the scales to immediately hit the ground. The sins were so strong that Winged Kuriboh turned black.
 
 Modern times 
   This section is  missing information  from the  Yu-Gi-Oh! GX  anime. 
 In the manga,  Koyo Hibiki  received the " Winged Kuriboh " card from  Maximillion Pegasus  at a  Duel Monsters  mall tournament, and passed it on to  Jaden Yuki . 
 In the  anime ,  Yugi Muto  gives it to Jaden after the latter bumped into the former in a big hurry. Yugi also said that he felt that the card wanted to be with Jaden. After obtaining the card, Winged Kuriboh became Jaden's  Spirit Partner  and often appeared alongside Jaden, particularly to express concern. Sometimes he would appear when Jaden needed him the most, like when he was in a tough situation, or to tell him the card he will draw could help him. In episode 4, Ms. Dorothy gave Jaden " Winged Kuriboh LV10 ", along with " Transcendent Wings ", to which he used to defeat Chazz. Winged Kuriboh will sometimes hide behind Jaden's head after being frightened by something. During season 3 Winged Kuriboh would sometimes fight with  Jesse Anderson's  spirit partner,  Ruby Carbuncle .
 
 Trivia 
 Winged Kuriboh, despite being one of Jaden's main spirit partners, never made an appearance in the movie  Bonds Beyond Time . However, his affinity with Winged Kuriboh is alluded to with his use of  The Flute of Summoning Kuriboh  during the Duel against Paradox.
