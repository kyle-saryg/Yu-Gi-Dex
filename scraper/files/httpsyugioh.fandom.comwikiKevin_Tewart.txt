 Kevin Tewart  (born 1970 or 1971) is  Konami 's current U.S. head of development for the  Yu-Gi-Oh!  Trading Card Game . He makes product announcements and, occasionally, ruling statements regarding the TCG. Before Konami took over distribution in Q4 2008, he worked at a top senior-level position for  Upper Deck Entertainment  on the TCG.
 Tewart used to frequent the pojo.biz boards under the name "KevinTewart". [1]

 Events 
 Tewart has often made appearances at events.
 
 Tewart and other members of UDE's Research and Development division used " The Seal of Orichalcos " during Orichalcos  Duel the Master  side events at  National Tournaments . 
 Attendees of the 2006  San Diego Comic-Con International  received the  San Diego Comic-Con 2006 promotional cards  by challenging Tewart,  Dan Scheidegger , or  Justin Reilly  to a  Duel . 
 Announcements 
 Tewart has made a number of announcements and ruling statements, including:
 
 Stating that the  Second National Tournament prize cards  (" Masahiro the Dark Clown ", " Yasushi the Skull Knight ", and " Tomohiro the Mountain Warrior ") are fake. [2] 
 Stating that  TCG promotional cards are not tournament-legal until their formal release , in response to the pre-release leak of " Crush Card Virus ". [ citation needed ] 
 Stating that  Super Short Print  isn't an actual rarity. [3] 
 Announcing the  Problem-Solving Card Text  update. [4] 
 Announcing a TCG rule change to how certain monsters like " Sangan " and " Reborn Tengu " interact with  Xyz Monsters . 
 In addition, Tewart occasionally writes articles for Konami's official  Yu-Gi-Oh! Trading Card Game  Strategy Site. [5] 
 
 Trivia 
 On May 16, 2005, Tewart confirmed he was currently 34 years old. [6] 
 On April 29, 2006, Tewart stated that his favorite artwork was that of " Mystic Swordsman LV6 ". [7] 
 On May 4, 2010, Tewart stated that he "dislikes" " Waboku ". [8] 
 Tewart confirmed that he started working on card effect wording after the release of  Pharaonic Guardian , starting with  Magician's Force . [9] [10] 
 Tewart has stated that he owned a copy of " The Seal of Orichalcos " and "Millennium Thousand-Eyes Virus", the latter a card that only he possessed. [11] [12]
