 Vampire Lord  is a character version of the card " Vampire Lord ". 
 He appears as a Super Partner available in  Yu-Gi-Oh! Duel Arena , with a default of 5 Rental Points. His partner ID is P118.
 He was only obtainable by having a  Yu-Gi-Oh! Duel Arena  account before maintenance on September 10, 2014, and accepting him as a gift before 30 days passed since he initially appeared in one's Gift Box. Beforehand, people with Facebook accounts could "Like" a  certain post  on Yu-Gi-Oh! BAM's Facebook page, accumulating rewards for everyone in-game based on the number of "Likes", up to a maximum of 5000 Likes. This "Like" period lasted from August 25, 2014 through August 31, 2014.
 Since the  end result  was 3355 "Likes", everyone in-game received 6710 Duel Points, a " Vampire Lord " card, and this character as a partner, following the maintenance on September 10, 2014. (Players would receive Duel Points equal to double the number of Likes the post ended up with. Achieving 5000 Likes would have awarded everyone in-game a " Vampire Vamp " card, along with the aforementioned rewards.)

 Description 
 Vampire King who rules the night. He has immortal body and spend his eternal life in  Vampire Empire .
 
 Deck 
 The  deck  mainly consists of  Zombie   type   monsters  having " Vampire " name on them. Zombie type monsters are good at  special summon  from  Graveyard .  Field Spell  has the  effect  not only to increase  ATK  but also to  destroy  your opponent's card by  sending  it from Deck to Graveyard.

 Trivia 
 This character's Deck name, along with some of its Side Deck cards, are references to the  Twilight  series by Stephenie Meyer.
 The Deck name "The Vampire Saga: Breaking Deck" is a parody of the movie titles  The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 1  and  The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 2 . 
 " Ghostrick Warwolf " in the Side Deck is a reference to werewolves being a major plot element of the Twilight series. 
 " Twilight Rose Knight " in the Side Deck has the word "Twilight" in its name. 
 The name " Tri-Wight " is a pun of "tri-", "wight" and "twilight".
 This reference is likely the reason why " The Lady in Wight " is in the Main Deck, and " Skull Servant " and " King of the Skull Servants " are in the Side Deck.
