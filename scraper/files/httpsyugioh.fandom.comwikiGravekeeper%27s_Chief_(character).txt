 Biography 
 Gravekeeper's Chief and his tribe lived in a ruins-based area of the  Duel Monsters Spirit World .  Lyman Banner  sent  Atticus Rhodes  to that dimension as a test. Tapping into the power of  Nightshroud , Atticus became the first person to defeat the chief. He was gifted with a one half of the  Shadow Charm  pendant as a result. [1] 
 Some time later, Banner repeated the process with  Jaden Yuki  when he encountered the Gravekeeper's Chief and his men. The Chief took  Alexis Rhodes ,  Syrus Truesdale ,  Chumley Huffington  and Banner himself hostage. They were placed at the bottom of the valley in sarcophagi where their heads are the only parts that are not mummified. Jaden and  Winged Kuriboh  are found by a group of guards and the Gravekeeper's Chief introduces himself. He declares his intention to execute them all by burying them alive, but agrees to pardon them if Jaden can defeat him in a challenge and he reveals a Deck. He engaged Jaden in a Duel, gaining an early advantage by locking down the  Graveyard  with " Necrovalley ". The sarcophagi would close as Jaden lost  Life Points . [2] 
 Jaden ultimately defeated him using " Elemental Hero Necroshade ", earning him the other half of the pendant. The rest of the tribe wanted to punish Jaden and his friends for trespassing despite the Gravekeeper's Chief telling them not to attack only for Jaden and his friends to receive help from  Yasmin . The guards part, and the group is allowed to leave, with Yasmin telling them that  Winged Kuriboh  will be able to lead them out. [1] 
 
 Non-canon appearances 
 Gravekeeper's Chief appears in Free Duel mode in the video game  Yu-Gi-Oh! 5D's World Championship 2009: Stardust Accelerator .
 
 Deck 
 Anime 
 In the anime, he plays a  " Gravekeeper's " Deck , which focuses on the wide range of abilities available with his "Gravekeeper's" cards, as well as " Necrovalley " which prevents both players from  activating  cards that interact with the  Graveyard .
